Research Group: Intelligent Security

Organizations implement more and more business processes with IT systems and process sensitive data by applications. The threat against such IT infrastructure is a well-known and steadily growing problem. Consistently, enterprises and public authorities are in the focus of hackers. Their motivation differs widely and ranges over stealing confidential data to gain profit, harming an s reputation due to political or financial reasons or may even be motivated by terrorism. As a necessary reaction in science, the research of effective countermeasures has came into the focus of researchers of two major areas: Information Security and Artificial Intelligence.

Intrusion Detection

A variety of systems have been presented in the recent years to detect network intrusions performed by hackers. These systems have been denoted Intrusion Detection Systems and are designed to fire an event in case of a detected malicious behavior which should be analyzed by a security officer. During the years, the complexity of IT infrastructures and, therefore, the amount of events from these systems has increased. Accordingly, the need for an efficient monitoring has grown. Additionally, enterprises are forced by governmental regularizations to ensure secured networks and data privacy which is summarized under the term IT compliance. A single product, such as an Intrusion Detection System or a firewall is not capable of this task. Security Information and Event Management (SIEM) systems, therefore, occurred on the market. These systems are managing events generated by products different such as Intrusion Detection Systems, firewalls, antivirus etc., programs and correlate enterprise relations between these events to detect possible threats. The improvement of the techniques used for this purpose is an essential goal of our research group.

Research areas covered: Machine Learning, Model Checking for Hard-/Software Safety, Network Security, Intrusion Detection
